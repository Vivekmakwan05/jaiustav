        DEMENTIA

  DEMENTIA

  • Dementia can be caused by brain cell death,and neurodegenerative disease,characterized by progressive and continuous loss of cognitive functions. There are Various underlying diseases or disorders that are the root cause of the syndrome. Dementia can be caused by a head Injury, a stroke, or a brain tumor. As the disorder progresses, the patient gradually becomes dependent on others to perform routine daily activities.
  • It is a broad category of brain diseases that cause a long term impact on impairment in memory, communication, and thinking.
  • The most common type of dementia are mentioned below.

  • Alzheimer's disease
  • Vascular dementia
  • Lewy body dementia
  • frontotemporal dementia
  • Post-traumatic dementia
  • Parkinson's
  • Normal pressure hydrocephalus 10.Creutzfeldt–Jakob disease
  • Stroke related Dementia
  • Alpha-synuclein disease
  • Chronic Traumatic Encephalopathy (Dementia Pugilistica)
  • Mild cognitive Impairment
  • Posterior cortical Atrophy
  • Ischemtic strokes
  • Mixed Dementia

        PALLIATIVE CARE

  PALLIATIVE CARE

  • Palliative care is "an approach that improves the quality of life of patients and their families facing the problems associated with life-threatening illness, through the prevention and relief of suffering by means of early identification and impeccable assessment and treatment of pain and other problems, physical, psychosocial, and spiritual. The goal of palliative care is, therefore, to improve the quality of life of both patients and families by responding to pain and other distressing physical symptoms, as well as to provide nursing care and psycho-social and spiritual support.
